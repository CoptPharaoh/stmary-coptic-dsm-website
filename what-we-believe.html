The Apostolic Faith

The Faith of the Apostles

The Coptic Orthodox Church is part of the One, Holy, Catholic, and Apostolic Church. Our faith is not a new invention, but the life and teaching handed down from Christ to the Apostles and preserved in the life of the Church.

This faith is guarded through Scripture, Holy Tradition, the Councils, the Fathers, the Liturgy, and the sacramental life of the Church.

01

Received from Christ

The Church receives her life and teaching from Our Lord Jesus Christ, who founded His Church and sent the Apostles into the world.

02

Handed Down

The Apostles preached, taught, baptized, ordained, and established communities that continued in one faith and worship.

03

Lived in the Church

Orthodox belief is not only studied. It is prayed, confessed, sung, received, and lived in the worshiping body of Christ.

The Holy Trinity

One God in Three Persons

We believe in one God: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. The Father is unbegotten, the Son is begotten of the Father before all ages, and the Holy Spirit proceeds from the Father.

The three Persons are one in essence, equal in glory, eternal, and undivided.

The Father

The Father is the source and Creator of all things seen and unseen.

The Son

The Son is eternally begotten of the Father, Light of Light and true God of true God.

The Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit is the Lord, the Giver of Life, worshiped and glorified with the Father and the Son.

One Essence

The Trinity is not three gods, but one God in three Persons, eternal and undivided.

Jesus Christ

The Incarnate Word of God

We believe that our Lord Jesus Christ is the eternal Son and Word of God, who became incarnate of the Holy Spirit and the Virgin Mary for our salvation.

Christ is fully divine and fully human. In Him, humanity is healed because the eternal Word of God truly entered our life, assumed our nature, and united it perfectly to Himself for our salvation.

Fully Divine and Fully Human

Our Lord Jesus Christ is not partly God and partly man. He is perfect in His divinity and perfect in His humanity, united in the one Person of Christ without confusion, without change, without division, and without separation.

Miaphysite Christology

As Oriental Orthodox Christians, we confess the Miaphysite Christology of St. Cyril of Alexandria: "one incarnate nature of God the Word." This does not deny Christ's humanity or His divinity, but confesses their perfect union in the one Person of Christ.

Salvation in Christ

Healing, Transformation, and Union with God

Salvation is not merely a legal declaration, but healing, transformation, and union with God through Christ.

01Christ Restores Humanity

By His Incarnation, Cross, Resurrection, and Ascension, Christ restores humanity, conquers death, forgives sin, and opens the way to eternal life.

02Grace, Faith, and Love

We are saved by grace through faith, and this living faith works through love. We do not earn salvation by our own power, but we cooperate with God's grace through repentance, faith, obedience, prayer, the sacraments, and a life of love.

The Church

The Body of Christ

The Church is the Body of Christ, the household of God, and the pillar and ground of the truth.

The Church is not merely a human organization but the living community where Christ is worshiped, the Gospel is proclaimed, the sacraments are received, and believers are formed in holiness.

The Church is where the Gospel is proclaimed, the mysteries are received, and the faithful are formed into the likeness of Christ.

The Life of the Church

The Holy Mysteries

The Sacraments and the Eucharist

The sacraments are true encounters with God's grace. Through the mysteries, the Church sanctifies the life of the faithful and joins us to the saving work of Christ. They are not symbolic reminders only, but holy actions through which Christ heals, nourishes, forgives, and strengthens His people.

B

Baptism

Through Baptism we are born again and united to Christ.

C

Chrismation

Through Chrismation we receive the Holy Spirit.

E

Eucharist

Through the Eucharist we receive the true Body and Blood of Christ.

R

Confession

Through Confession we receive forgiveness, healing, and reconciliation.

The Eucharist

We believe the Eucharist is truly the Body and Blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, not merely a symbol. In the Divine Liturgy, the faithful participate in the heavenly worship and are united to Christ.

Scripture and Holy Tradition

The Living Faith Handed Down

The Holy Bible is the inspired Word of God. The Church receives, preserves, interprets, and lives the Scriptures within Holy Tradition.

Tradition is not human custom replacing Scripture, but the living faith of the Church handed down from the Apostles.

Holy Scripture

The Bible is read, chanted, preached, and interpreted within the worship and teaching life of the Church.

Holy Tradition

Tradition is the apostolic life of the Church: the faith received, preserved, and lived by the Body of Christ.

Saints and Intercession

The Communion of Saints

The saints are members of the Body of Christ who have finished the race and now live with Him. We honor them, learn from their lives, and ask their prayers.

We worship God alone. Asking the saints for their prayers is not worship; it is the Church's confession that the Body of Christ is alive, united, and prayerful before God.

The saints are alive in Christ and intercede before God. Their lives encourage us, their prayers strengthen us, and their witness reminds us that holiness is possible by the grace of God.

Matthew 22:32

"God is not the God of the dead, but of the living."

Hebrews 12:1

We are surrounded by "so great a cloud of witnesses" as we run the race set before us.

Revelation 5:8

The heavenly worship includes golden bowls full of incense, "which are the prayers of the saints."

Revelation 8:3-4

The prayers of the saints rise before God with incense in the heavenly sanctuary.

Luke 15:7

There is joy in heaven over one sinner who repents.

2 Maccabees 15:14

In the Deuterocanonical books received in Orthodox tradition, the righteous are shown praying for God's people.

St. Mary the Theotokos

The Mother of God

We honor St. Mary as the Theotokos, the Mother of God, because the One born from her is truly God Incarnate.

She is the greatest among the saints, the model of obedience, humility, purity, and faithful discipleship.

Prayer and Repentance

A Life of Continual Transformation

Orthodox Christian life is a life of continual repentance and transformation. Prayer, fasting, almsgiving, confession, Scripture, and participation in the Liturgy help shape us into the likeness of Christ.

P

Prayer

Prayer turns the heart toward God in praise, repentance, thanksgiving, and intercession.

F

Fasting

Fasting trains the body and heart in humility, watchfulness, and love.

A

Almsgiving

Mercy toward others teaches us to love as Christ loves.

L

Liturgy

The worship of the Church forms us in Scripture, prayer, and sacramental life.

Resurrection and Eternal Life

The Hope of the Coming Age

We believe in the resurrection of the dead and the life of the age to come.

Christ has conquered death, and those united to Him await the resurrection and the fullness of His Kingdom.

The Nicene Creed

The Nicene Creed

The Nicene Creed is the universal statement of faith confessed by Orthodox Christians throughout the world. It was formulated by the First and Second Ecumenical Councils and remains the Church's concise confession of the Holy Trinity, the Incarnation of Christ, the Church, Baptism, the Resurrection, and the life of the age to come.

The Father

Truly we believe in one God, God the Father the Pantocrator, Creator of heaven and earth, and of all things seen and unseen.

The Church begins with the one God, the Father almighty, Creator and source of all life.

The Son

We believe in one Lord Jesus Christ, the Only-Begotten Son of God, begotten of the Father before all ages; Light of Light, true God of true God, begotten not created, of one essence with the Father; by Whom all things were made; Who, for us men and for our salvation, came down from heaven, and was incarnate of the Holy Spirit and of the Virgin Mary, and became man. And He was crucified for us under Pontius Pilate, suffered and was buried. And on the third day He rose from the dead, according to the Scriptures. He ascended into the heavens; He sits at the right hand of His Father; and He is coming again in His glory to judge the living and the dead; Whose kingdom shall have no end.

The Creed confesses the full divinity and true humanity of Jesus Christ, His incarnation, Cross, Resurrection, Ascension, and coming judgment.

The Holy Spirit

Yes, we believe in the Holy Spirit, the Lord, the Giver of Life, Who proceeds from the Father; Who, with the Father and the Son, is worshiped and glorified; Who spoke by the prophets.

The Holy Spirit gives life, speaks through the prophets, and is worshiped with the Father and the Son.

The Church

And in one, holy, catholic, and apostolic Church. We confess one baptism for the remission of sins.

The Church is the household of God, united in the apostolic faith and in the sacramental life of Christ.

The Resurrection

We look for the resurrection of the dead, and the life of the coming age. Amen.

Christian hope is not an idea only, but resurrection, renewal, and eternal life in the Kingdom of God.
