What Is Repentance?

A Return to God

Repentance is a return to God - a complete change of heart, mind, and direction. It is the first step in our healing and restoration.

Through repentance, we reject sin and reorient our lives toward Christ's love, grace, and truth.

Why Is Repentance Essential?

The Beginning of Healing

Jesus began His ministry by calling people to repentance. Repentance restores communion with God, opens the heart to grace, and bears fruit in a transformed life.

Repentance is not despair. It is a hope-filled return to the Father.

Matthew 4:17

"Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand."

Acts 3:19

"Repent therefore and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out."

Repentance Is Not Despair

Sorrow Joined to Hope

Repentance is not self-hatred, hopeless guilt, or fear without hope. True repentance is sorrow joined to trust in the mercy of God.

The enemy uses guilt to drive us away from God, but the Holy Spirit convicts us in order to heal us and bring us home.

"A broken and a humbled heart God shall not despise."

Psalm 50

The Prodigal Son

The Father Runs to Meet Us

The Prodigal Son shows the heart of repentance: awakening, confession, return, and the Father's embrace.

The son returns saying, "Father, I have sinned," and the father runs to meet him with compassion. This is the image of true repentance: not simply feeling guilty, but rising and returning home.

Psalm 50

The Great Prayer of Repentance

Psalm 50 is the great prayer of repentance in the Coptic Orthodox Church. It teaches us to ask for mercy, cleansing, renewal, humility, and a restored heart.

"Create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit within me."

Psalm 50

"No sin is so great that it can conquer the munificence of the Master. Even if one is a fornicator, or an adulterer... the power of the gift and the love of the Master are great enough to make all these sins disappear and to make the sinner shine more brightly than the rays of the sun."

Steps Toward Repentance

Returning with the Whole Life

01

Awareness

Acknowledge the sin and its effect on your soul and others.

02

Contrition

Have genuine sorrow for having sinned against God.

03

Confession

Bring your sins to the priest in the Sacrament of Confession.

04

Amendment

Firmly intend not to return to sin, trusting in God's grace.

05

Fruits of Repentance

Live a changed life marked by humility, prayer, love, and obedience.

Prayer for Repentance

A Prayer Returning to Mercy

My Lord, God and Savior Jesus Christ, treasure of mercy and spring of salvation, I come to You confessing my sins. I confess that with boldness I dared to defile Your holy sanctuary with my sins. Now I seek Your mercy and love, for Your mercies are boundless; You never turn back a sinner who comes to You. I confess that my mind is burdened with sin and that I have no strength left. Do not turn away from me; do not rebuke me in Your anger nor chasten me in Your displeasure. I am worn out; have compassion upon me, O Lord. Do not judge me according to Your justice, but according to Your mercy. Remember Your creation; do not put me on trial, because none of Your servants can justify their deeds. Dress me in a new attire that befits Your glory. Forgive my sins and I shall sing, "Blessed is he whose sins are forgiven." When I confess my sins and reveal my iniquities, You cleanse me. Amen.

Spiritual Practices

Remedies for Healing, Growth, Humility, and Watchfulness

Spiritual exercises are remedies for healing, growth, humility, and watchfulness. They should be practiced with prayer, discernment, and the guidance of a confession father.

Spiritual practices help repentance become a lived reality rather than only words said in confession. They are not punishments, but remedies for the healing of the soul. They help the repentant grow in grace, resist sin, and become more watchful.

Spiritual exercises should not be practiced pridefully or without discernment. They are best received with the blessing and guidance of one's confession father, so that spiritual growth remains humble, balanced, and safe.

Ecclesiastes 4:10

"Woe to him who is alone when he falls..."

Proverbs 11:14

"Where there is no counsel, the people fall; but in the multitude of counselors there is safety."

Proverbs 5:12-13

"How I have hated instruction, and my heart despised correction... I have not obeyed the voice of my teachers."

Why Spiritual Practices Matter

Many people desire spiritual growth but need practical exercises to help them struggle faithfully. Without follow-up, confession can become routine - words spoken and absolution received - without lasting transformation.

Spiritual practices help the repentant cooperate with God's grace through prayer, watchfulness, obedience, and practical change. A person should not invent spiritual exercises carelessly or out of pride. The devil can use inexperience to push a person into pride, despair, or imbalance. For this reason, the guidance of a confession father is important.

Examples of Spiritual Exercises

Against Personal Weaknesses

For sins of personal weakness, such as lying, swearing, gossiping, anger, or judging others, a confession father may give a simple exercise such as prostrations, prayer, apology, silence, or another practice that helps the person become watchful.

From the Ten Commandments

  • Remember the day of the Lord and keep it holy.
  • Honor your father and mother.
  • Do not murder.
  • Do not commit adultery, either in action or in thought.
  • Do not steal or take what is not yours.
  • Do not bear false witness, lie, or deceive.
  • Do not covet what belongs to another.

From the Sermon on the Mount

  • Practice humility and spiritual poverty.
  • Practice meekness toward all people.
  • Show mercy to the needy.
  • Struggle for purity of heart and simplicity.
  • Be a peacemaker, not a troublemaker.
  • Bear tribulation and false accusation with patience for God's sake.
  • Avoid provoking hostility with others.
  • Learn to give, even if it is only a little.
  • Love your enemies, pray for them, and help them in times of need.
  • Give, pray, and fast in secret.
  • Trust in God as the lilies of the field and the birds of the air.
  • Guard your tongue from judgment, gossip, and harmful speech.
  • Persevere in prayer: ask, seek, and knock.
  • Follow the golden rule: do unto others what you would have them do unto you.

Practicing Love

  • Love suffers long and is kind: bear with others kindly.
  • Love does not envy: rejoice in the good of others.
  • Love does not parade itself: do not boast.
  • Love is not puffed up: fight pride through humility.
  • Love does not behave rudely: speak gently and decently.
  • Love does not seek its own: avoid selfishness and learn sacrifice.
  • Love is not provoked: struggle against anger.
  • Love thinks no evil: assume good and keep a pure mind.
  • Love does not rejoice in iniquity: do not delight in another's fall.
  • Love rejoices in the truth: rejoice with those who rejoice and weep with those who weep.
  • Love bears all things: practice patience.
  • Love believes all things: keep simplicity and trust without being naive.
  • Love hopes all things: hope for the good and salvation of others.
  • Love endures all things: remain steadfast through trials.
  • Love never fails: true love stands firm through testing.

"Rejoice with those who rejoice, and weep with those who weep."

Romans 12:15

"Love is as strong as death... Many waters cannot quench love."

Song of Songs 8:6-7

Specific Spiritual Exercises

H

Humility

  • Do not provoke people to anger.
  • Be quiet and peaceful in speech and actions.
  • Do not interfere unnecessarily in the affairs of others.
  • Practice being a good listener.
  • Listen patiently, even to those younger or lower in position.
  • Do not complain to anyone except God, and do so with humility.
  • Practice cheerfulness with all people.
  • Be obedient, wise, and flexible.
P

Prayer

  • Practice reverence during prayer: reverence of heart, body, and senses.
  • Learn the Psalms, Gospels, and prayers of the Agpeya gradually.
  • Practice praying throughout the day.
  • Repeat the Jesus Prayer: "O my Lord Jesus Christ, have mercy on me, a sinner."
  • Pray for others and show love to all.
  • Pray for enemies and for those who insult you.
F

Fasting

  • Be committed to the fasts of the Church with care.
  • Practice an appropriate period of abstinence, according to your ability and guidance.
  • Be moderate with fasting foods.
  • Let fasting lead to prayer, repentance, humility, and love.
R

Reading

  • Daily reading of the Bible is very important.
  • Read Scripture regularly, not only as a habit but with attention.
  • Keep a pen and notebook nearby to record helpful verses and thoughts.
  • Mark verses that touch your heart.
  • Choose one verse to remember throughout the day.
  • Record practical verses that can be lived, such as "Do not judge," "Love without hypocrisy," "Bless and do not curse," and "Rejoice with those who rejoice, and weep with those who weep."
  • Try to practice what you read, so Scripture becomes life.

Conditions for the Success of Spiritual Exercises

01

Clarity

The exercise should be clear, not vague or confusing.

02

Specificity

The exercise should focus on a specific struggle or virtue, such as guarding the tongue, avoiding judgment, practicing prayer, or fasting with moderation.

03

Proper Timing

The exercise should fit the circumstances. Silence may not be practical during an important family gathering, and fasting must be practiced wisely.

04

Gradual Progression

Spiritual growth should be gradual. Sudden extreme practices can lead to pride, exhaustion, or discouragement.

05

Duration

Exercises need time to bear fruit. A short-lived practice may not produce lasting transformation.

06

Conviction

The person should understand the need and benefit of the exercise.

07

Prayer

The person must ask God for strength, because no spiritual practice bears fruit without grace.

08

Guidance

The prayers and guidance of the confession father help the repentant remain balanced and consistent.

09

A Spiritual Notebook

A notebook can help record guidance, verses, struggles, progress, and practical steps for growth.

The goal of every spiritual exercise is not pride, self-reliance, or outward appearance, but healing, humility, watchfulness, and union with Christ.

Amen.
