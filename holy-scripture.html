The Word of God in the Church

Scripture at the Heart of Orthodox Life

The Holy Scriptures are inspired by God and are central to the life of the Church. They are read in worship, prayed in the Agpeya, proclaimed in the Divine Liturgy, studied by the faithful, and interpreted according to the mind of the Church.

01

Read in Worship

The Scriptures are proclaimed publicly in the Church's services and feasts.

02

Prayed Daily

The Psalms and biblical prayers shape the daily prayer life of Orthodox Christians.

03

Lived in Christ

The goal of Scripture is communion with Christ, repentance, and transformation.

Which Came First?

The Church or the Bible?

The Church did not come from a printed Bible. The Church was born on Pentecost, preached the Gospel, celebrated the Eucharist, baptized believers, and preserved the apostolic faith before the New Testament canon was formally recognized.

The same Church that received and preserved the Scriptures also teaches us how to interpret them.

The Scriptures belong to the worshiping Church that received them, guarded them, and lives by them.

The Apostolic Life of the Church

Scripture and Holy Tradition

The Living Apostolic Faith

Holy Tradition is not human custom replacing Scripture. It is the living apostolic faith of the Church. Scripture is the written witness of that faith, and Tradition is the life of the Church in which Scripture is rightly received and understood.

Scripture

The written Word of God, inspired by the Holy Spirit and received by the Church for worship, teaching, correction, and formation.

Tradition

The living faith handed down from the Apostles in worship, doctrine, holiness, sacramental life, and the witness of the Fathers.

The Canon of Scripture

Received and Recognized by the Church

The canon of the Bible was recognized by the Church over time. The early Church read the apostolic writings, preserved them, tested them according to apostolic faith, and received them for worship and teaching.

Old Testament

The Old Testament Scriptures were received from the worshiping community of Israel and read by the Church in the light of Christ.

New Testament

The New Testament writings came from the apostolic Church and bear witness to Christ and the life of His Body.

Councils and Fathers

The Councils and Fathers witnessed to the canon and guarded the Church's faithful reading of Scripture.

Deuterocanonical Books

The Coptic Orthodox Church receives the Deuterocanonical books as part of the Old Testament tradition.

Scripture in the Divine Liturgy

The Bible Proclaimed in Worship

The Coptic Liturgy is filled with Scripture: Pauline Epistle, Catholic Epistle, Acts, Synaxarium, Psalms, Gospel, and many prayers shaped by biblical language.

P

Pauline Epistle

The apostolic teaching of St. Paul is read to the faithful.

C

Catholic Epistle

The universal epistles call the Church to faithfulness and holiness.

A

Acts

The work of the Holy Spirit in the early Church is proclaimed.

G

Gospel

The words and works of Christ stand at the center of the Liturgy.

Scripture in Prayer

Praying the Bible

The Agpeya prayers are saturated with Psalms and Scripture. Orthodox Christians do not only study Scripture; they pray it.

Through repeated prayer, the words of Scripture become the language of repentance, praise, thanksgiving, and hope.

How to Read the Bible as an Orthodox Christian

Read Prayerfully and Within the Church

P

Read Prayerfully

Begin with prayer, asking God to illumine the heart and mind.

H

Read with Humility

Approach Scripture to be formed by God, not merely to win arguments.

C

Read Within the Church

Receive Scripture with the worshiping Body that preserved it.

F

Read with the Fathers

Let the saints and teachers of the Church guide faithful interpretation.

X

Read Christ-Centered

All Scripture leads us to Christ, the fulfillment of the Law and Prophets.

L

Read for Love

Let Scripture lead to repentance, worship, mercy, and love.
