What Is Confession?

Repentance Brought Before Christ

Confession is the holy Mystery where repentance is brought before God in the presence of the priest, and the absolution is prayed. We receive God's forgiveness and healing through honest confession, spiritual guidance, and Christ's mercy.

Repentance is the inner turning of the heart back to God; Confession is the sacramental act by which that repentance is confessed before God in the presence of the priest and healed through absolution.

Why Confess to a Priest?

Mercy Received in the Life of the Church

We confess to God in the presence of the priest. Christ gave His apostles authority to bind and loose sins, and this authority continues in the apostolic priesthood of the Church.

Confession gives spiritual healing, accountability, counsel, and absolution. The priest keeps confidentiality and guides the soul toward repentance, not shame. He stands as a servant of Christ's mercy and a witness before God.

How to Prepare for Confession

Come with Hope, Not Despair

Preparation helps the heart come honestly before God. The goal is not anxiety or self-condemnation, but clarity, humility, and trust in Christ's mercy.

01

Pray Psalm 50

Begin with repentance and ask God to create in you a clean heart.

02

Examine Honestly

Look at your conscience before God without excuses or despair.

03

Write Privately

If helpful, write sins down privately so you can speak clearly.

04

Be Concise

Tell the truth plainly, without unnecessary details or self-defense.

05

Do Not Blame

Confession is about your sins and healing, not proving others wrong.

06

Ask for Courage

Pray for humility, honesty, and renewed hope in Christ.

How Confession Is Practiced

The Shape of the Mystery

The person confesses before God in the presence of the priest. The priest listens, offers guidance, and may give spiritual exercises. The person bows their head, the priest prays the absolution, and the person leaves with thanksgiving, watchfulness, and renewed hope.

1

Confession

The repentant person names their sins before God in the presence of the priest.

2

Guidance

The priest offers counsel and helps the soul turn toward healing.

3

Absolution

The priest prays the absolution through the authority Christ entrusted to His Church.

4

Renewal

The person leaves with gratitude, vigilance, and hope in God's mercy.

Priests, Bishops, and the Pope Also Confess

No One Is Above Repentance

No one in the Church is above repentance. Priests, bishops, and even the Pope have confession fathers. A priest also confesses because he too must live a life of repentance and receive spiritual guidance.

This shows that confession is not humiliation but healing, and that all members of the Church stand in need of God's mercy.

The Absolution Prayer

Christ Forgives and Restores

After confession, the priest prays the absolution over the repentant person. The priest does not forgive by his own power. Christ forgives through the grace of the Holy Spirit and the authority entrusted to the Church.

Master, Lord Jesus Christ, the Only-begotten Son and Logos of God the Father, who has broken every bond of our sins through His saving, life-giving sufferings, who breathed into the face of His holy disciples and saintly apostles, and said to them, "Receive the Holy Spirit. Whose sins you will remit, they are remitted to them, and those which you will retain, they shall be retained."

You also now, our Master, through Your holy apostles, have given grace to those who for a time labored in priesthood in Your Holy Church, to forgive sin upon the earth and to bind and to loose every bond of iniquity.

Now, also, we ask and entreat Your goodness, O Lover of Mankind, for Your servants, my fathers, and my brethren, and my weakness, those who bow their heads before Your holy glory. Dispense unto us Your mercy, and loose every bond of our sins.

And if we have committed any sin against You, knowingly or unknowingly, or through anguish of heart, or in deed, or in word, or from faint-heartedness, You, O Master, who knows the weakness of men, as a good and loving God, grant us the forgiveness of our sins.

Bless us, purify us, absolve us, and absolve Your servant. Fill us with Your fear, and straighten us unto Your holy, good will. For You are our God, and all glory, honor, dominion, and adoration are due unto You, together with Your good Father and the Holy Spirit, the Life-giver, who is of one essence with You, now and at all times and unto the age of all ages. Amen.

From the Third Absolution

The priest prays in humility, often using "we" and "us," showing that he also stands in need of God's mercy. The absolution is not the priest acting by his own power; it is Christ forgiving through the grace of the Holy Spirit in His Church.

Prayers Before and After Confession

These prayers help the heart approach Christ with honesty, humility, and hope.

Prayer Before Confession

O Holy Father, who desires the repentance of sinners, promising to accept them back; look now, O Lord, at my sinful soul for I have erred and lost my way in the valleys of transgressions for many years, and I am embittered and wretched for being removed from the fountain of my salvation.

Yet, now I come to You, asking to be cleansed from all defilement and filth. Do not reject, but accept me. Look at me with compassion and deal with me mercifully and I will be cleansed and saved; do not neglect me lest I perish.

Grant me grace that I may approach You with faith and hope, to confess my trespasses and my sins. Enlighten my heart that I may realize my sins, my mistakes and my straying. Grant me determination to forsake evil that I may be established in Your commandments and live for the glory of Your holy name. Amen.

Prayer After Confession

I thank Your goodness, O Lover of Mankind because You did not desire that I perish, but have awakened me from my slumber and guided me to Your path. You saved me from the valley of destruction, and brought me to the safety of Your bosom.

Fill me with hope and faith, for I have come to You as the sick one to his physician, and as the hungry to the Spring of living water, as the poor to the Source of riches, as the sinner to the Savior, and as the dead to the Fountain of Life; for You are my salvation, my healer, my life, my strength, my comfort, my joy, and my rest is in You.

Help me, protect me, and teach me to put my will in Your hands, that I may live according to Your will. Remove my weakness that I may be firm and continue to be faithful to You until the end. Amen.

Examination of Conscience

Questions for Honest Reflection

These categories can help you prepare without becoming overwhelmed. Bring what is true before God and ask Him for healing.

01

Relationship with God

Have I trusted God, loved Him, and sought His will?

02

Prayer and Worship

Have I neglected prayer, Scripture, the Liturgy, or attentiveness in worship?

03

Love for Others

Have I served others with patience, mercy, and humility?

04

Words and Anger

Have my words wounded, judged, lied, gossiped, or stirred anger?

05

Purity of Heart

Have I guarded my thoughts, desires, eyes, and actions?

06

Honesty and Responsibility

Have I been faithful in work, family, promises, money, and duties?

07

Forgiveness

Have I refused forgiveness or kept bitterness in my heart?

08

Spiritual Laziness

Have I delayed repentance or neglected opportunities for growth?
