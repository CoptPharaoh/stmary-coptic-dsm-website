What Is Baptism?

New Birth in Christ

Baptism is the first of the holy Mysteries through which a person is spiritually reborn, adopted by God, and united with the Body of Christ - the Church. It is not a mere symbol but an actual cleansing, renewal, and transformation through the Holy Spirit.

01

Reborn

Through Baptism, the faithful enter new life in Christ by water and the Spirit.

02

Cleansed

Baptism washes away sin and begins the baptized person's life of repentance and holiness.

03

United

The baptized person is joined to Christ and welcomed into His Church.

Why Is Baptism Necessary?

The Gateway to Salvation and the Church

Baptism is necessary because Christ Himself teaches that we must be born of water and the Spirit. It is the beginning of sacramental life and the gateway into the Church.

R

Spiritual Rebirth

It is a spiritual rebirth and gateway to salvation.

C

Cleansing from Sin

It washes away both original sin and personal sins committed before baptism.

U

United to Christ

It unites us to the death and resurrection of Christ.

A

Adoption

It grants adoption as children of God and entrance into the Church.

S

Prepared for Chrismation

It prepares us to receive the Holy Spirit through Chrismation.

Biblical Foundation

Born of Water and the Spirit

The Church's teaching on Baptism is rooted in the words of Christ and the apostolic witness of the New Testament.

John 3:5

"Most assuredly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God."

Matthew 28:19

"Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."

Acts 2:38

"Repent, and let every one of you be baptized in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins; and you shall receive the gift of the Holy Spirit."

Romans 6:3-4

"Do you not know that as many of us as were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into His death? Therefore we were buried with Him through baptism into death, that just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life."

1 Peter 3:21

"There is also an antitype which now saves us - baptism (not the removal of the filth of the flesh, but the answer of a good conscience toward God), through the resurrection of Jesus Christ."

Church Fathers on Baptism

The Ancient Witness of the Church

The Fathers speak of Baptism as rebirth, cleansing, grace, illumination, and entrance into the life of Christ.

St. Irenaeus

"He came to save all through means of Himself - all, I say, who through Him are born again to God."

Origen

"The Church received from the apostles the tradition of giving baptism even to infants."

St. Cyprian of Carthage

"The mercy and grace of God is to be denied to no human being born."

St. Augustine

"Baptism washes away all sins, absolutely all sins, whether of deed, word, or thought."

St. Gregory of Nazianzus

"Baptism is God's most beautiful and magnificent gift."

How Baptism Is Performed

The Order of the Mystery

In the Coptic Orthodox Church, Baptism is celebrated reverently and sacramentally as the person is joined to Christ and His Church.

3

Triple Immersion

It is performed by a priest using triple immersion in consecrated water.

T

The Holy Trinity

The priest invokes the Holy Trinity: "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit."

C

Chrismation

Baptism is followed immediately by Chrismation, the seal and gift of the Holy Spirit.

E

Eucharist

The newly baptized is received into Eucharistic life, according to the practice of the Church.

P

Preparation

Infants of Orthodox families are baptized early; adult converts undergo catechism and confession first.
