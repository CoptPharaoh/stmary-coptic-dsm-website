What Is Chrismation?

The Seal of the Holy Spirit

The Sacrament of Chrismation, also called Confirmation, is the seal of the Holy Spirit given to a person immediately after Baptism. Through anointing with the Holy Myron oil by a priest, the newly baptized receives the gift of the Holy Spirit and is fully united with the Body of Christ.

It is practiced immediately after Baptism as part of the Apostolic Tradition of the Church.

01

Sealed

The baptized person receives the seal and gift of the Holy Spirit.

02

Anointed

The priest anoints the newly baptized with the consecrated Holy Myron.

03

United

The newly baptized is fully united to the sacramental life of the Church.

Church Fathers on Chrismation

The Ancient Witness of the Church

St. Cyril of Jerusalem

"Having been baptized into Christ and put on Christ, you have been made partakers of Christ. But since Christ is also anointed with oil, it is fitting that after baptism you also be anointed. For as the sharing of His death is realized in baptism, so also the sharing of the Holy Spirit is given through chrism."

St. Ephrem the Syrian

"In baptism the body is washed, that the soul may be cleansed in the anointing with oil."

St. Ambrose of Milan

"You were anointed on the forehead... for the reason that you may know the grace of Christ."

How Chrismation Is Performed

The Order of the Mystery

1

After Baptism

Immediately after Baptism, the priest anoints the newly baptized with Holy Myron.

2

The Whole Person

The anointing is placed on various parts of the body.

3

The Seal

The priest says, "The seal of the gift of the Holy Spirit."

4

Apostolic Continuity

This anointing continues the apostolic laying on of hands mentioned in Acts.

5

Holy Communion

The newly baptized is now a full member of the Church and may receive Holy Communion.

Early Church Practice

Baptism and Chrismation Belong Together

From the earliest times, the Orthodox Church practiced Chrismation immediately after Baptism. The Apostles laid hands on the baptized to impart the Holy Spirit.

As the Church spread and bishops could not be physically present everywhere, this apostolic practice was preserved through the consecrated Holy Myron, blessed by the bishops and used by priests in the Church.

The Holy Myron

Consecrated Oil and the Unity of the Church

The Holy Myron is consecrated oil used in Chrismation. It is prepared and consecrated by the bishops of the Church, showing the unity of the newly baptized with the whole apostolic Church.

Through this anointing, the baptized person receives the seal of the Holy Spirit and is strengthened to live the Christian life.
